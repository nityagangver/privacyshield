Membership Inference Attack Demo

What is a Membership Inference Attack?

A Membership Inference Attack (MIA) exploits the confidence difference of a machine learning model. Because standard models adapt tightly to their training datasets, they predict known members with significantly higher confidence than unseen ones. An attacker can analyze these confidence margins to accurately guess whether a specific user's private record was used to train the model.

About PrivacyShield

What is Differential Privacy?

Differential Privacy (DP) is a rigorous mathematical framework that guarantees the privacy of individuals in a dataset. By injecting calibrated noise during model training (e.g., using diffprivlib's differentially private algorithms), the model learns aggregate population patterns without memorizing the specific values of any individual's record.

What is Epsilon (ε)?

Epsilon (ε) represents the "privacy budget." A lower ε means stronger privacy protection because more noise is added, making it harder to leak details. Conversely, a higher ε adds less noise, yielding a model closer to baseline performance but with weaker mathematical privacy guarantees. An ε of 1.0 represents the optimal sweet spot.
